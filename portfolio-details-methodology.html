Introduction to Streaming

What is Streaming?

Multimedia that is continuously given and consumed from a source is known as streaming media. Streaming is a way of broadcast where video or audio by the internet are being transmitted simultaneously, usually live, and where viewers can watch in real time. Rather than the information itself, streaming refers to the way that it is delivered. As one of the main forms of entertainment for the majority of people on the internet, streaming or live streaming has grown in popularity over the last few years.

What are the Pros and Cons of Live Streaming?

Pros
  • Unlike other pre-recorded materials, live streaming is easier. One can stream raw footage without worrying about editing and doing several takes;
  • As one of the leading forms of entertainment for the majority on the internet, live streaming has grown in popularity over the last few years. As a result, there are a lot of social platforms where streamers can stream freely without making their viewers sign up on a platform they are not a member of and;
  • Live streaming is a great way to engage and interact with viewers since this offers real-time engagement and entertainment to everyone watching.
Cons
  • As a streamer, you might face technical errors, glitches, audio errors, and buffering that might affect your performance as a streamer and may affect the engagement of your viewers;
  • In some platforms, streams won't be saved after it ends that could be a downside as new followers can't be able to go back and watch your past live streams and;
  • It would be tough to know when to start a stream people who are following you might be on the other side of the world, where there is a different time zone.

What makes a good stream?

There are a variety of characteristics that makes a good stream, depending on the kind of content you're focusing on, but the following factors are essential for all kinds of content:
  • Good Video Quality: A high-quality camera necessitates that the stream is viewable in a respectable manner for the viewer.
  • Good Audio Quality: The audio transmitted from the server to the client must be audible.
  • Consistency: Setting a stream schedule could improve one's audience engagement.
  • Accessibility: All viewers' devices and locales must be compatible with one's stream to enhance the number of audiences who will watch it.
